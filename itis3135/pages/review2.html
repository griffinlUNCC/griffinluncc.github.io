Overall Review

All boxes are checked for the most part, with the sole exception being the use of times new roman on the ITIS-3135 page.

As for the design itself, the use of colors is good and each page is both distinct and unique. On the ITSC-3135 page there is a small issue with the titlebar, where it overlaps with itself, but otherwise all pages are designed well, passing all validation tests and using CRAP fairly well. My only other suggestion would be a clearer visual separation between the two titlebars, as especially at smaller page resolutions that leads to wrapping and overlap.

For the "Learn Python" site, there is some issue with whitespace where content is all left-aligned, and capped to a certain width, which works at smaller sizes, but at larger viewport widths leads to an unbalanced webpage. It is otherwise informative, with all pages that are well designed, containing content that is spaced well and properly split between pages.

The issues above are not at all present on the hobby site, which is incredibly well designed, using a strong color palette, good hierarchy for content importance, and balances content well based on readability.

I think the sites are overall very well designed, and follow CRAP principles for the most part, with small misses listed above. The code and file organization is very well done and meets all standards. All expected sites and elements are present!
